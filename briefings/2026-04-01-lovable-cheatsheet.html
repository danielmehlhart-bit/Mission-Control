Your First Build.
No code required.
This cheat sheet gets you from "I've never built anything" to a working first prototype — in one afternoon. Follow the steps, use the prompts, and remember: the tool does the heavy lifting.
⏱ Estimated time to first working prototype: 30–90 minutes
💡
The one thing you need to know
You do not need to know how to code. You need to know how to describe. Think of Lovable like a very fast junior developer — it needs clear instructions, not technical expertise. Your job is to explain what you want. The tool's job is to build it. If it builds the wrong thing, you describe it better. That's it.

Two tools. One simple workflow.

Step 1 · Think
ChatGPT or Claude
Your thinking partner
Use this before you open Lovable. Describe your topic in plain language, let it ask you questions, and use that back-and-forth to get clarity on what you actually want to build. When you're done, you'll have a clear brief — ready to paste into Lovable.
Step 2 · Build
Lovable
Your builder — lovable.dev
Lovable turns your description into a real, working app — complete with a shareable link. No installation, no code. You describe, it builds. You react, it improves. After 3–4 iterations you'll have something you can actually share with your team.

Your first build in 4 steps
Follow this exactly for your first project
1
Define what you want to build
ChatGPT or Claude · 15 min
Open ChatGPT or Claude and type: "I work at Drooms, a B2B SaaS company for secure data rooms. I want to build [your topic]. Help me define what the simplest first version should look like. Ask me questions." Let it ask. Answer honestly. By the end, copy the summary and use it as your Lovable prompt.
2
Open Lovable and paste your prompt
Lovable · 5 min
Go to lovable.dev, sign in, and start a new project. Paste the description you got from Step 1 directly into the chat — or use one of the example prompts below. Hit enter. Lovable will start building. Don't panic if it doesn't look perfect immediately — that's normal.
3
Give feedback, iterate
Lovable · 20–40 min
Look at what it built. Then describe what's missing or wrong in plain English — like you're talking to a person: "The button should be on the right side", "Add a column for status", "Remove the sidebar". Do 3–5 rounds of this. Your first real output will emerge.
4
Share the link
Lovable · 2 min
Lovable gives every project a shareable URL — no installation needed. Copy it and send it to Daniel or your team with one line: "Here's my first version — feedback welcome." That's your first build. Done.

Do's & Don'ts
The most common mistakes — and how to avoid them
✅ Do this
  • ✓
    Start small. One screen, one use case. You can always add more.
  • ✓
    Describe what you see, not what's wrong technically. "The button is in the wrong place" is better than "the CSS layout is broken."
  • ✓
    Iterate fast. 5 small feedback rounds beat one long, detailed brief.
  • ✓
    Share early. An imperfect working link beats a perfect description of what you plan to build.
  • ✓
    Use Claude/GPT to pre-think. If you're unsure what to build, spend 15 minutes with it before opening Lovable.
🚫 Avoid this
  • ✗
    Don't wait for perfect clarity. The prompt doesn't have to be perfect. Start, see what comes out, adjust.
  • ✗
    Don't try to build everything at once. "Build me a full CRM with reporting and integrations" will produce a mess. Scope it down.
  • ✗
    Don't give up after one bad output. One unclear instruction = one bad result. Rephrase and try again. It's not broken.
  • ✗
    Don't put real customer data into prototypes. These tools are for internal use only — use dummy data or anonymized examples.
  • ✗
    Don't skip the thinking step. Opening Lovable without knowing what you want leads to frustration. 10 minutes with Claude first saves 45 minutes of confusion.
🧱
When you're stuck — here's what to do
  • It built something completely wrong: Don't start over. Describe specifically what's wrong: "Remove the sidebar. Make the header dark. Show only three columns, not five."
  • You don't know what to build: Open ChatGPT/Claude and say: "I have to deliver [your Q2 deliverable] by [date]. What's the simplest tool or prototype I could build with Lovable that would help?" Follow the conversation.
  • It keeps making the same mistake: Try adding "Important:" before your instruction. Or start a fresh message and re-describe the full context from scratch.
  • You feel like it's too complicated: That means you're trying to build too much. Take one feature and describe only that. One table. One form. One page. Ship that, then add.

The right mindset for building with AI
This is different from using ChatGPT for questions
🗣️
You're the director
The AI builds. You decide what to build, give feedback, and steer. Your judgment is the most important ingredient.
🔄
Iteration is the method
Don't expect perfection on the first try. Three rounds of feedback beat one perfect brief. Move fast, react, improve.
🚢
Ship beats perfect
A working prototype you can share today is more valuable than a polished idea you're still refining in two weeks.
