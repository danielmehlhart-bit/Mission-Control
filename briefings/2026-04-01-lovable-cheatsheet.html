Your First Build.
No code required.
This cheat sheet gets you from "I've never built anything" to a working first prototype — in one afternoon. Follow the steps, use the prompts, and remember: the tool does the heavy lifting.
⏱ Estimated time to first working prototype: 120–180 minutes
💡
The one thing you need to know
You do not need to know how to code. You need to know how to describe. Think of Lovable like a very fast junior developer — it needs clear instructions, not technical expertise. Your job is to explain what you want. The tool's job is to build it. If it builds the wrong thing, you describe it better. That's the whole process.
🧰
Two tools. One simple workflow.
Use them in sequence — think first, then build
Step 1 · Think
ChatGPT or Claude
Your thinking partner
Use this before you open Lovable. Describe your topic in plain language, let it ask you questions, and use that back-and-forth to get clarity on what you want to build. When you're done, you'll have a clear brief — ready to paste into Lovable.
Step 2 · Build
Lovable
Your builder — lovable.dev
Lovable turns your description into a real, working app — complete with a shareable link. No installation, no code. You describe, it builds. You react, it improves. After 3–4 iterations you'll have something you can actually share with your team.

Your first build in 4 steps
Follow this exactly for your first project
1
Define what you want to build
ChatGPT or Claude · 15 min
Open ChatGPT or Claude and type: "I work at Drooms, a B2B SaaS company for secure data rooms. I want to build [your topic]. Help me define what the simplest first version should look like. Ask me questions." Let it ask. Answer honestly. By the end, copy the summary — that's your Lovable prompt.
2
Open Lovable and paste your prompt
Lovable · 5 min
Go to lovable.dev, sign in, and start a new project. Paste the description from Step 1 — or use one of the example prompts below. Hit enter. Lovable starts building. Don't panic if it doesn't look perfect immediately — that's completely normal.
3
Give feedback, iterate
Lovable · 20–40 min
Look at what it built. Describe what's missing or wrong in plain English — like talking to a person: "The button should be on the right side", "Add a column for status", "Remove the sidebar." Do 3–5 rounds. Your first real output will emerge. See the Pro Tips section below for how to do this even more precisely.
4
Share the link
Lovable · 2 min
Lovable gives every project a shareable URL — no installation needed. Copy it and send it to Daniel or your team with one line: "Here's my first version — feedback welcome." Done. That's your first build.

Plan Mode vs. Build Mode — always plan first
The most important Lovable feature most beginners skip
🗺️ Plan Mode — use this first
Plan Mode
Shortcut: Alt + P  ·  or the map icon in the chat bar
In Plan Mode, Lovable thinks out loud — it shows you exactly what it plans to build before writing a single line of code. You can review, correct misunderstandings, and refine the direction. No credits are used.

Use it whenever you are about to ask for something new or complex: a new feature, a layout change, anything with multiple moving parts. Think of it as asking a contractor to show you the blueprint before they start building.
How to activate
Press Alt + P — or click the small map icon in the bottom-left of the chat input. Type your request. Lovable shows a step-by-step plan. Review it, adjust if needed, then confirm to start building.
⚡ Build Mode — use for small tweaks
Build Mode
Default — Lovable builds immediately when you hit enter
Build Mode is the default. Lovable immediately starts writing code based on your message. Fast and great for small, clear changes where you know exactly what you want.

The risk: if your request is vague or complex, Lovable can go in the wrong direction and you spend time correcting instead of building. For anything beyond a tiny tweak — switch to Plan Mode first.
⚠️ Build Mode without a clear plan = the most common beginner mistake. When in doubt, use Plan Mode first.
💡
The rule: Plan Mode for anything new or complex. Build Mode only for small, obvious tweaks.
Adding a new section, changing a layout, describing something with multiple parts? Open Plan Mode first. Read what it plans to do — does it match what you meant? If yes, confirm. If not, correct before any code is written. This one habit saves more time than anything else on this page.

Pro Tips — how to get better results, faster
These make the difference between a frustrating session and a great one
🖼️
Biggest lever
Paste a screenshot — show, don't tell
Found an app or website that looks the way you want yours to look? Take a screenshot and paste it directly into Lovable's chat alongside your prompt. The AI interprets visuals far better than written descriptions. This is the single fastest way to get the right design on the first try.
How to say it
"Build something that looks similar to this screenshot — same clean card layout, dark header, same spacing feel. Here's what I need it to do: [your description]."
@
Precision editing
Use @mention to point at components
When you want to change something specific — a button, a card, a header — don't describe it vaguely. In Lovable's chat, type @ and it shows you a list of components in your project. Click the right one, then describe your change. This tells Lovable exactly where to look instead of guessing.
How to say it
"@HeroSection — change the background to dark navy and make the title font larger. Keep everything else the same."
🎯
Better prompts
Be hyper-specific about one thing at a time
Asking for five changes at once leads to messy results. Pick the most important thing, describe it precisely, then review what changed before the next instruction. Think of it as a conversation — one clear ask per turn. Precision beats length.
Weak vs. strong
❌ "Make it look better and add more stuff."
✅ "Add a status column to the table with three values: Active, Pending, Done. Show them as colored badges."
📋
Better context
Give context upfront, not after things go wrong
The more Lovable knows about your goal from the start, the fewer corrections you'll need. Mention who uses the tool, what problem it solves, and what "done" looks like. A 3-sentence context paragraph at the start saves 10 correction rounds later.
How to say it
"This is for our internal sales team at Drooms (B2B SaaS, ~30 reps). They need to quickly find training resources. The goal is fast access, not fancy features. Keep it simple."
🔎
Design inspiration
Find references before you start
Spend 5 minutes on dribbble.com or mobbin.com searching for UI that looks like what you want. Screenshot it. Paste it into your first prompt. You don't need to design anything yourself — just show the AI what good looks like. Your taste is the input; the AI does the execution.
How to find good references
Search Dribbble for: "dashboard clean minimal" or "SaaS internal tool" — pick something that feels right.
📐
Planning saves time
80% planning, 20% building
The biggest mistake is opening Lovable before knowing what you want. Spend more time clarifying the idea than you think is necessary — in Claude, on paper, wherever. If you can't describe your tool in 3 sentences, the plan isn't ready yet. Build time is fast once the thinking is clear.
The 3-sentence test
"This tool is for [who]. It solves [what problem]. The first version only needs to do [one thing]."
🔧
When something goes wrong — what to do
In order. Try each step once before moving to the next.
1
Let Lovable fix it itself
Click the "Try to fix" button if it appears. The AI often catches its own errors. Try this once — if it doesn't work, move on.
2
Describe exactly what's wrong
Tell Lovable specifically what you see vs. what you expected: "The table is showing blank rows. It should show the 3 items I added." Specific beats vague, every time.
3
Use @mention to isolate the problem
Type @ in Lovable's chat, select the component that's broken, and describe just that component. Narrowing the scope helps the AI focus and avoids breaking other things.
4
Revert and re-prompt
Lovable has a version history. Go back 2–3 steps to a version that worked, then re-describe what you want — more clearly this time. Sometimes starting clean is faster than trying to fix.
5
Ask Daniel
If you've been stuck for more than 20 minutes, reach out. That's not a failure — it's how this works in a team. Share the Lovable link and describe what you were trying to do.
⚠️ After any stuck moment, ask Lovable: "How could I have described this better?" Use the answer to improve your next prompt. You're learning a new language — every session you get better at it.

Do's & Don'ts
The most common mistakes — and how to avoid them
✅ Do this
  • ✓
    Paste screenshots. Show Lovable what you want — visuals beat words every time.
  • ✓
    Start small. One screen, one use case. You can always add more later.
  • ✓
    Use @mention when targeting a specific component. Precision beats guessing.
  • ✓
    Iterate fast. 5 small feedback rounds beat one long, detailed brief.
  • ✓
    Share early. An imperfect link beats a perfect idea you haven't shipped yet.
  • ✓
    Pre-think in Claude/GPT. 15 minutes there saves 45 minutes of Lovable confusion.
🚫 Avoid this
  • ✗
    Don't wait for perfect clarity. Start, see what comes out, adjust from there.
  • ✗
    Don't ask for 5 changes at once. One clear ask per message gets cleaner results.
  • ✗
    Don't give up after one bad output. One unclear prompt = one bad result. Rephrase and try again.
  • ✗
    Don't use real customer data in prototypes. Always use dummy or anonymized data.
  • ✗
    Don't skip the thinking step. Opening Lovable without a clear goal leads to frustration.
